Species Profile

Trachypithecus leucocephalus

The white-headed langur is a critically endangered species of langur endemic to the karst limestone hills of southern Guangxi province, China. This striking primate is characterized by its black body contrasted with a distinctive white head, shoulders, and the lower half of its tail.

Physical Characteristics

  • Body length: 50-70 cm
  • Tail length: 60-80 cm
  • Weight: 8-10 kg
  • Distinctive white head and shoulders

Behavior & Habitat

  • Diurnal and arboreal
  • Lives in groups of 2-31 individuals
  • Feeds primarily on leaves, fruits, and flowers
  • Inhabits karst limestone forests

Conservation Status: Critically Endangered (IUCN Red List). Only about 1,400 individuals remain in the wild.

Endemic to China

Found only in southern Guangxi province

Conservation Initiatives

Discover the ongoing efforts to protect and preserve the white-headed langur and its unique karst habitat.

Protected Areas

The Chongzuo White-headed Langur National Nature Reserve was established to provide a safe haven for these primates. The reserve covers over 25,000 hectares of critical habitat.

Smart Monitoring

AI-powered camera traps and remote video surveillance systems monitor langur populations around the clock, providing valuable data for conservation management.

Over 500 acres of langur habitat have been restored, including the creation of ecological corridors, water sources, and food plantations to support the growing population.

A Conservation Success Story

Once on the brink of extinction with only 300 individuals remaining in the 1980s, the white-headed langur population has rebounded to over 1,400 today. This remarkable recovery is a testament to the effectiveness of targeted conservation efforts.

Through habitat protection, anti-poaching measures, community engagement, and scientific monitoring, we've created a model for endangered species recovery that can be applied worldwide.

Key Locations

Chongzuo White-headed Langur National Nature Reserve

The largest protected area for white-headed langurs, covering 25,578 hectares and home to approximately 94% of the total population.

Guangxi Zhuang Autonomous Region

Nonggang National Nature Reserve

A UNESCO Biosphere Reserve that provides critical habitat for approximately 6% of the white-headed langur population.

Fusui Rare and Precious Animal Nature Reserve

A key protected area that supports a small but important population of white-headed langurs.
